Watch Overview

In our increasingly digital world, financial institutions such as banks and investment companies rely heavily on technology to manage sensitive data and transactions. With the rise of online banking, mobile apps, and digital payments, the need for robust cybersecurity has become more critical than ever. Cybersecurity in financial institutions refers to the protection of computer systems, networks, and data from cyber threats like hacking, phishing, and ransomware attacks. Without strong security measures, both the institution and its customers are at serious risk.

Discussion

Financial institutions are a prime target for cybercriminals because they store massive amounts of personal and financial data. If this data is stolen, it can be used for identity theft, financial fraud, and other crimes. Common cyber threats in this industry include:

Phishing attacks

Cybercriminals send fake emails or create fraudulent websites to trick employees or customers into revealing sensitive login credentials. This can lead to unauthorized access to accounts and data breaches.

Malware & Ransomware

Hackers use malicious software to infiltrate financial systems, encrypting data or stealing information. They often demand ransom payments, which disrupts operations and damages customer trust.

Data Breaches

When unauthorized users gain access to confidential financial data, it can result in identity theft, fraud, legal issues, and loss of customer confidence, causing long-term harm to the institution.

Insider Threats

Employees or contractors might accidentally or deliberately leak sensitive information. Strong access controls and regular training help mitigate these risks and protect critical data.

Capital One Data Breach (2019)

In July 2019, Capital One experienced a significant data breach affecting over 100 million customers. A former Amazon Web Services employee exploited a misconfigured firewall to access sensitive data, including names, addresses, credit scores, and social security numbers. The breach highlighted vulnerabilities in cloud infrastructure and the importance of proper security configurations.

Bangladesh Bank Heist (2016)

In February 2016, hackers attempted to steal nearly $1 billion from Bangladesh Bank's account at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York. They successfully transferred $81 million to accounts in the Philippines by exploiting the SWIFT banking system. A typographical error in one of the transfer requests raised suspicions, preventing further losses. Investigations linked the heist to North Korean hacking groups.

Reflection

Cybersecurity in financial institutions is not just a technical issue—it’s a matter of trust. People rely on banks and financial apps to manage their money, often without thinking twice. But every transaction carries a risk if not properly protected. Learning about this topic made me realize how even small security mistakes can lead to huge financial losses and loss of trust. It also shows that cybersecurity is a shared responsibility between institutions and users. I believe cybersecurity should be treated as a priority, not just an IT task, but a core part of how a financial institution operates every single day.
